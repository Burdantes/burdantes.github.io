When RR has no informative candidate

Traceroute reveals new candidates; RevTr tries RR on each one before considering symmetry.

1. Regular tracerouteS → K → J → P → R6 reveals three new candidates.
2. Try RR againRR to P, J, and K also yields no informative reverse hop.
3. Check AS boundaryR6-P and P-J remain within one AS; J-K crosses into another.
4. Assume Symmetry (Intradomain)Reverse R6 → P → J. Reject Assume Symmetry (Interdomain) on J → K.
Trust rule: Assume Symmetry (Intradomain) one link at a time; reject Assume Symmetry (Interdomain).

RevTr 2.0 chooses M-Lab vantage points close to destination-prefix ingresses. If RR still cannot reveal a hop, it uses regular traceroute and keeps one link at a time only when both ends are in the same AS; an interdomain link causes the measurement to abort.
